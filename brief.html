AI Safety Law-a-Thon 2.0

A weekend working event pairing practising lawyers with AI safety researchers in small teams, to turn frontier-AI risks into concrete legal arguments — liability theories, contract clauses and courtroom-ready reasoning.

First edition: October 2025, hosted at LISA, London — 140 registrations, in-person seats sold out. This edition is the finale of the AI Liability Course (August–September 2026).

The Event

DatesSaturday 5 – Sunday 6 September 2026
backup: 12–13 September
Hours10:00–18:00 · setup from 09:00
Attendanceapprox. 60–80 participants, plus a small organising team
Formatteams of 3–5 at tables; short plenary presentations; judged submissions
OrganiserAI Plans (ai-plans.com), an AI-safety research non-profit

Venue Requirements

  • One main space seating the full group at tables, with a screen for presentations
  • Reliable wifi and power at tables
  • A small serving area — we arrange our own catering (a simple lunch and refreshments)
  • Exclusive use of the hired space for the duration of the event
Hosting Terms We Propose As a small mission-driven organisation, we partner with community venues wherever possible. In return we offer: a donation to the venue, full setup and teardown by our team, and public credit as event host before an audience of lawyers, researchers and AI-policy professionals.
